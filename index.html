Why does period not depend on amplitude? ▶
In SHM, F=−kx means force ∝ displacement. A bigger push gives a bigger restoring force in exact proportion — so the motion speeds up to match, leaving T=2π√(m/k) unchanged. This isochronism is what makes pendulum clocks accurate.
Why does heavy mass oscillate slower? ▶
Newton's 2nd law: F=ma. Same spring force, but more mass means less acceleration. Less acceleration → takes longer to complete each cycle. T∝√m — doubling mass increases period by √2≈1.41×.
What exactly is critical damping? ▶
At ζ=1, the characteristic equation has a repeated root. The solution x=(C₁+C₂t)e^(−ω₀t) decays fastest without oscillating. Underdamped oscillates (ζ<1); overdamped decays slower (ζ>1). Critical is the crossover point — ideal for car suspensions and instrument needles.
Why is the phase portrait an ellipse? ▶
Conservation of energy: ½mv²+½kx²=E means (x/A)²+(v/Aω₀)²=1 — a standard ellipse. The axes are x (position) and v (velocity). Damping spirals it inward because energy leaks out each cycle.
Why does stiffer spring → faster oscillation? ▶
Stiffer spring = stronger restoring force for same displacement. That larger force accelerates the mass faster back toward equilibrium, completing each cycle in less time. ω₀=√(k/m) — frequency grows as √k.

2 Drag the mass to different amplitudes. Confirm the period doesn't change — only the amplitude does. ○
3 Try Critical damping preset. Compare how fast it returns vs Underdamped and Overdamped. ○
4 Double the mass. Does the period increase by √2 (≈1.41×)? Check the ω₀ readout before and after. ○
5 Open the Phase tab in live graphs. With no damping, trace the perfect ellipse. Add damping to see it spiral inward. ○

Why is 45° the best angle? ▶
R=v₀²sin(2θ)/g. This is maximised when sin(2θ)=1, so 2θ=90°, giving θ=45°. It perfectly balances time-of-flight (needs high angle) vs horizontal speed (needs low angle).
Why does horizontal speed stay constant? ▶
Newton's 1st law: no force = no acceleration. With no air resistance, nothing acts horizontally, so vₓ = v₀cosθ forever. Gravity only acts vertically.
Why is the path a parabola? ▶
From x=vₓt, we get t=x/vₓ. Substituting into y: y = ax + bx² — a quadratic (parabola) in x. This only holds when g is constant (flat Earth, no drag).
Why do 30° and 60° give the same range? ▶
sin(2×30°)=sin(60°) and sin(2×60°)=sin(120°)=sin(60°). So R(30°)=R(60°). Generally, complementary angles θ and 90°−θ always give the same range.
Why does doubling v₀ quadruple range? ▶
R ∝ v₀². This is because doubling speed: (1) doubles time of flight (via vᵧ∝v₀), and (2) doubles horizontal speed (vₓ∝v₀). Two factors of 2 multiply: 2×2=4×.

〰 Speed is fixed by medium
▶
Changing f always changes λ to keep v = λf constant. Speed is a property of the medium (tension/density), not the wave. Try doubling f — wavelength halves, v unchanged.

Why doesn't the string travel with the wave? ▶
The string carries energy, not mass. Each point only oscillates up and down locally. The moving pattern is a shape traveling through the medium — think of ripples on water, the water doesn't flow sideways.
Why does doubling f halve wavelength? ▶
Wave speed v is fixed by the medium (tension, density). Since v = λf, if f doubles and v stays constant, λ must halve: λ = v/f. More oscillations per second → crests are more tightly packed.
How do two waves create a standing wave? ▶
By superposition: y₁ = A·sin(kx−ωt) + y₂ = A·sin(kx+ωt). Using the sum-to-product identity: y = 2A·sin(kx)·cos(ωt). The sin(kx) factor sets fixed node positions; cos(ωt) makes the whole pattern oscillate in time.
Why are only certain frequencies allowed (harmonics)? ▶
Fixed ends must be nodes (y=0 always). This requires sin(kL)=0, so kL=nπ, giving λₙ=2L/n. Only wavelengths that fit an integer number of half-waves within the string's length are allowed.
Why is amplitude squared proportional to energy? ▶
Wave energy ∝ (max velocity)² ∝ (Aω)² ∝ A². Each point oscillates like a simple harmonic oscillator — and for SHM, E=½kA². Doubling amplitude → 4× the energy. This is why loud sounds require much more power.

⚡ Inverse-square insight
▶
Field strength drops as 1/r². This comes from spreading influence over the surface of a growing sphere (area=4πr²). Both gravity and electrostatics obey this for the same geometric reason.

Why do field lines never cross? ▶
At any point in space there is exactly one unique electric field direction. If two lines crossed, there would be two directions simultaneously — a contradiction. The field (and hence lines) is a single-valued vector function.
Why does field obey 1/r² and not 1/r? ▶
The total field "flux" from a charge is conserved. It spreads over the surface of a sphere with area 4πr². Since total flux stays constant and area ∝ r², field strength must drop as 1/r². This 3D geometry argument applies to gravity too.
Why do like charges repel and unlike attract? ▶
In Coulomb's law F=kₑq₁q₂/r², the sign of force depends on the product q₁q₂. Same signs → positive product → repulsive. Opposite signs → negative product → attractive. This follows from the quantum exchange of photons at a deeper level.
What is a test charge and why must it be tiny? ▶
E⃗ = F⃗/q₀ in the limit q₀→0. A real large charge would rearrange nearby charges, distorting the field it's trying to measure. An infinitesimal test charge probes the field without disturbing it. It's positive by convention — setting the direction of field lines.
Why does a dipole field drop faster (1/r³)? ▶
A dipole has zero net charge — the 1/r² fields from +q and −q partially cancel. The leading term cancels exactly, leaving a weaker 1/r³ residual. At large distances the charges look like a single neutral object. This is why water molecules' fields weaken quickly.
